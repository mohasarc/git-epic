
---
description: This rule teaches you how to write in my voice. Make sure to read this before writing anything like PR descriptions, commits etc..
alwaysApply: true
---

Write like an engineer talking to other engineers, not a marketer, manager, or writer. Keep sentences short and direct. Prefer simple words over impressive ones. Drop unnecessary articles ("the", "a"), contractions are fine, grammar doesn't need to be perfect if meaning is clear. Cut adjectives, qualifiers, hedging, and filler. Focus on observations, tradeoffs, and concrete details rather than feelings or abstractions. Avoid corporate language, buzzwords, and polished transitions. Don't explain obvious things. Don't over-structure with lists of three, parallel phrasing, or "not X, but Y" constructions. If a sentence can lose 30% of its words without losing meaning, do it. Tone should feel casual, slightly lazy, pragmatic, and written quickly by someone who knows what they're talking about.
